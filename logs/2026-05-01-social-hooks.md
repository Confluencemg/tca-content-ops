# Secondary insights — for IG carousel slides and standalone posts
secondary_insights:
  - The three honest questions a chart can answer are: Where are buyers and sellers actually meeting? How much conviction is behind the last move? And where will I know I'm wrong?
  - Technical analysis is risk management with a measuring tape — it makes the "I was wrong" price visible before you enter, so you can size the position to the loss you can tolerate.
  - The chart cannot tell you why price moved. Causality lives outside the chart, which is why the trader who confuses correlation in price with explanation gets punished the first time the usual pattern doesn't hold.
  - Chart patterns are a poor map for retirement decisions. Time horizon, withdrawal needs, tax structure, and the gap between essential expenses and guaranteed income don't show up on a candlestick chart.
  - Same chart, two outcomes: a part-time trader uses it to size risk and place a stop; a soon-to-be retiree uses it to time multi-quarter moves in her 401(k). The skill isn't different. The question is.

# Contrarian angle — Thomas's stance against conventional wisdom
contrarian_angle: |
  The popular framing of technical analysis is binary — either it works (and you can read the
  market's mind) or it doesn't (and it's noise). Thomas's stance is that it works, but for a
  much smaller list of jobs than its loudest defenders claim. The most expensive misuse of
  technical analysis isn't a bad trade. It's a retirement plan reorganized around a chart pattern.
