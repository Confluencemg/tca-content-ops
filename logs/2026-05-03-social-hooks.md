# Lead insight — the single strongest standalone takeaway

lead_insight: |
  The biggest fear in retirement isn't running out of money. It's the bad year

  withdrawal strategies aren't. The Now, Soon, and Later buckets exist so that

# Secondary insights — for IG carousel slides and standalone posts
secondary_insights:
  - The Now bucket is one to two years of spending in cash and short-term cash-equivalents — high-yield savings, money market, short Treasury. Its only job is liquidity.
  - The Soon bucket is the income floor — Social Security, pension if you have one, and a Fixed Index Annuity sized for income (not for growth). The cash flow is guaranteed by the structure of the instrument, not by hopes about the market.
  - The Later bucket is your stock allocation. It only works as a growth engine if you don't have to sell out of it during a bad market. The Now and Soon buckets exist so you don't have to.
  - The "X percent rule" approach asks you to sell shares while they're cheap when a bad sequence hits early. That permanently shrinks the portfolio for year twelve. Bucket planning sidesteps the question.
  - When you have buckets, the Later bucket can drop 25% and your spending doesn't move. The number on the statement no longer tells you whether you can buy groceries. Same portfolio, different relationship.

# Contrarian angle — Thomas's stance against conventional wisdom
