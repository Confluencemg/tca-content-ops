lead_insight: |
  Most families assume the will decides who inherits the money. For the biggest

  doesn't. The beneficiary form on file with the custodian wins, every time.

  will the same family paid an attorney to write.

# Secondary insights — for IG carousel slides and standalone posts
secondary_insights:
  - Retirement accounts, life insurance, annuities, and TOD/POD accounts pass by beneficiary designation, not by your will. The form on file is the contract; the institution does not consult the will.
  - The three patterns that cause the most damage are the stale ex-spouse designation, the blank or "my estate" line, and the missing contingent beneficiary. All three are paperwork problems, not financial-strategy problems.
  - Federal law preempts state ex-spouse revocation rules for ERISA plans like most 401(k)s — even if your state automatically removes an ex on divorce, the federal plan does not. The form still wins.
  - When an account routes through your estate by default, you lose the tax efficiency a named individual beneficiary would have used. The will may still distribute correctly, but the channel got more expensive.
  - The audit is short — every IRA, every employer plan, every life insurance policy, every annuity, every HSA, every TOD/POD account, plus 529 successor owners. Confirm primary, contingent, percentages, and that names match current reality.
