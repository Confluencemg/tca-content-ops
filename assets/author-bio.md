# Author Bio Block

Canonical author bio that appears at the end of every TCA blog post and on lead magnet back matter. Inserted after the standard blog footer disclaimer.

The bio establishes Thomas's professional credibility (Series 65 IAR) without crossing into advisory-services CTA territory. It frames Thomas as the author and publisher, not the advisor — consistent with the standalone publishing posture.

---

## Where this gets applied

- **Blog posts:** required, immediately after the standard blog footer disclaimer block.
- **Lead magnet PDFs:** required, on the "About the Author" page in front or back matter.
- **Newsletter:** required, in the footer of every issue (after the disclaimer block).
- **Product back matter (Just in Case Binder, future products):** required, on the "About the Author" page.
- **Social media:** the bio in this file is the source of truth for platform "About" / "Bio" sections. Adapt for character limits but keep the credential line and CMG framing intact.

## What this bio does NOT include

These were considered and excluded:

- **No "Fiduciary Advisor" credential.** That phrase is being phased off the homepage. Bio uses the regulatory-accurate "Series 65 Investment Advisor Representative."
- **No advisory CTA.** "Schedule a free consultation" or Calendly links are not part of the editorial author bio — the editorial publishing is structurally separate from the advisory practice.
- **No firm affiliations.** "Of Altitude Capital Management" is correct on advisory-side disclosures (already on the site footer), but not appropriate in the editorial author bio. The author bio is about Thomas the writer and publisher.
- **No "20 years of experience" puffery.** "Nearly two decades" is the calibrated phrase — accurate without being marketing-y.
- **No client claims.** "Has helped hundreds of families" or similar aggregate claims are SEC Marketing Rule trip-wires for advisory content, and brand-inappropriate for editorial.
