# Compliance Filter — Mandatory Final Pass

Run this checklist on every piece of long-form sales copy before delivery. Flag any item that fails. Do not deliver copy that fails any item without flagging it explicitly to Thomas.

## Context

Thomas Clark is a Series 65 Investment Advisor Representative. Confluence Media Group LLC is a structurally separate publishing entity under Confluence Financial Group LLC. The product copy this skill produces is for CMG publishing products.

The compliance posture: CMG products are standalone publishing products by an author who happens to be a financial professional. They are not advertisements for advisory services under the SEC Marketing Rule. This separation must be visible and durable in all public-facing copy.

The risk surfaces are:
1. SEC Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1) — applies to investment advisers' advertisements
2. State-level IAR rules in Thomas's registration jurisdictions
3. Firm-level compliance review (Altitude Capital Management baseline)
4. Reputational risk with the demographic that buys these products

This filter addresses all four.

## Pass/Fail Checklist

### 1. Advisory Funnel Language
**FAIL if any of these appear:**
- "Schedule a call" / "Book a consultation" / "Speak with our team"
- "See if you qualify for [advisory service]"
- "Learn how we help clients"
- "Discover if working with us is right for you"
- "Apply to work with [Thomas / our firm]"
- Any CTA that drives readers to advisory intake or discovery calls
- Any language framing the product as a "first step" or "introduction" to advisory services

**PASS examples:**
- "Get the binder" / "Buy now" / "Add to cart"
- "Join the community" / "Subscribe"
- "Get the book" / "Order"
- "Read more on the blog" / "Watch the next video"

All CTAs route to publishing-business destinations only.

### 2. Author Bio and Credential Disclosure
**FAIL if:**
- Series 65 IAR credential is presented as the reason to buy the product
- Bio reads like an advisor pitch ("I help clients with...")
- Bio includes language that converts into solicitation ("schedule time with me to learn more")
- AUM, client outcomes, or advisory firm performance is mentioned anywhere

**PASS examples:**
- "Thomas Clark is a Series 65 Investment Advisor Representative with 18+ years of experience in retirement planning. He created [product] to help families..."
- "Background: Series 65 IAR, founder of Confluence Media Group, author of..."
- Any framing where the credential establishes author authority for the publishing product, similar to a physician-author disclosing MD credentials in a health book

The credential is disclosure, not solicitation.

### 3. Performance Claims and Implied Promises
**FAIL if any of these appear:**
- "Retire 5 years earlier"
- "Increase your retirement income by X%"
- "Avoid losses in the next market crash"
- "Get guaranteed returns"
- "What if you could [specific financial outcome]?"
- Any specific dollar amount the buyer will "save" or "earn" by using the product
- Any backtested or hypothetical performance illustrations
- Any claim about market conditions or future returns

**PASS examples:**
- "The binder organizes everything your family needs to know about your finances in one place" (describes what the product DOES, not what outcomes it produces)
- "Includes a Social Security worksheet that walks through claiming considerations" (describes content, not promised results)
- "Helps families have organized conversations about money" (describes function, not financial outcomes)

Rule: describe what the product IS and what it CONTAINS. Do not describe what financial outcomes it produces.

### 4. Testimonials and Social Proof
**FAIL if:**
- Testimonials appear without proper disclosures (Marketing Rule requires specific disclosures even for legitimate testimonials when the speaker is an IAR)
- Testimonials describe specific advisory outcomes Thomas produced
- Testimonials read as endorsements of Thomas as an advisor
- "5,000 happy clients" or similar aggregate claims without substantiation

**PASS examples for product reviews (with caution):**
- Verified buyer reviews of the publishing product itself, properly attributed, with disclosure that reviews are from buyers of the publishing product (not advisory clients)
- Generic praise for the product's utility ("This binder is exactly what my family needed")
- Author endorsements from other professionals about the BOOK or PRODUCT, not the advisor

When in doubt, omit. The default for TCA product pages should be light on testimonials and heavy on clear product description.

### 5. Manufactured Scarcity and Urgency
**FAIL if:**
- "Only 7 spots left!" (when not literally true)
- Countdown timers that reset
- "Price goes up at midnight!" (when it doesn't)
- "Limited time only!" without a real limit
- Fake "members are joining now" notifications

**PASS examples:**
- Genuine launch pricing with a real end date
- Limited cohort sizes that are actually limited
- Pre-order pricing that's actually a pre-order
- Honest framing: "I'm offering launch pricing through [date] because I want early adopters" — direct, true, not manipulative

Honesty is the test. Real scarcity is fine. Manufactured scarcity is a Marketing Rule problem AND erodes brand trust with this audience.

### 6. Doomsday and Fear-Based Hooks
**FAIL if:**
- Headlines about "the coming crash"
- "Dollar collapse" / "currency crisis" / "hyperinflation" framing
- "Government coming for your retirement" type political-financial hooks
- "Banks don't want you to know..." conspiracy framing
- Anything that uses fear of macro economic events as the primary motivator

**PASS examples:**
- Genuine education about real risks ("Sequence-of-returns risk is real and worth planning for")
- Honest framing of why the product exists ("Most families haven't organized their financial information, and that creates real problems when something happens")
- Specific, defensible problem statements

The binder solves a real problem. That problem doesn't need to be inflated into apocalypse.

### 7. Required Disclaimers
**FAIL if:**
- Page is missing the standard educational content disclaimer
- Page is missing identification of Confluence Media Group LLC as the publisher
- Page conflates CMG with Confluence Capital Management or Confluence Financial Group in a way that suggests advisory services
- Required state disclosures for Thomas's IAR registration are absent if the page is determined to be advertising-adjacent

**PASS:**
- Footer includes: educational content disclaimer, publisher identification, separation language between CMG and advisory entities
- Disclaimers are written to match the Altitude-reviewed baseline language already approved for thomasclarkadvisor.com
- Author bio includes appropriate credential disclosure without solicitation

When uncertain about disclaimer wording, default to the baseline language Altitude has already approved for the blog and adapt to the product context.

### 8. Brand and Aesthetic Tells
**FAIL if:**
- Copy reads as info-marketer hype (regardless of whether it's technically compliant)
- Tone matches the Agora financial publishing aesthetic
- Page would feel out of place next to a Vanguard or Morningstar product
- Page would feel at home next to a Stansberry or Banyan Hill VSL

This is a brand and demographic test, not a regulatory one — but it matters as much as the regulatory items. The audience that buys TCA products has a finely tuned bullshit detector. Hype destroys conversion with this demographic.

## Reporting

After running the checklist, deliver one of three outputs:

**CLEAN** — All items pass. Copy delivered with no compliance flags.

**FLAGGED** — One or more items fail. List specifically which items fail, where they appear in the copy, and the suggested fix. Do not deliver the copy as final until Thomas reviews flags.

**ESCALATE** — Fundamental positioning issue that the copy can't fix without restructuring the underlying offer. Surface to Thomas with a recommendation. Examples: a product that's been positioned in a way that inherently functions as advisory solicitation, or a launch concept that requires testimonial structures that won't pass scrutiny.
