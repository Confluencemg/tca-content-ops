# Curated Swipe Files

Sources to model STRUCTURE from when writing TCA sales copy. NOT to copy from. The principle is the same one Halbert taught: study what's already converted, learn the patterns, apply them in your own voice and context.

## Why this list excludes the obvious

Brunson's recommended swipe files (Agora Publishing VSLs from Banyan Hill, Oxford Club, Paradigm Press, Stansberry) are deliberately excluded. Reasons:

1. Agora's financial publishing operations have been the subject of major SEC and FTC enforcement action over exactly the marketing patterns those VSLs use
2. Implied performance promises, fabricated track records, manufactured scarcity, and doomsday hooks would violate the SEC Marketing Rule for a Series 65 IAR
3. The aesthetic actively repels Thomas's actual demographic — pre-retirees with real money have been burned by that style and recognize it instantly
4. Even if regulatorily safe, it would erode the brand's credibility with the audience that matters

The swipe files below come from operators who have built nine-figure businesses in financial education, family-organization, and adjacent regulated-but-not-securities spaces — without the Agora playbook.

## Tier 1: Direct-fit swipe files for TCA products

### For the Just in Case Binder and family-organization products

**Trustworthy.com** (trustworthy.com)
What to model: their "Family Operating System" positioning, the way they describe what's inside without overpromising, the calm visual register of the entire site.
Particularly useful: their landing page hero, their "Who it's for" section, their FAQ structure.

**Everplans** (everplans.com)
What to model: how they handle the death-and-incapacity topic without being morbid or frightening. The empathetic-pragmatic tone is exactly the register the binder needs.
Particularly useful: their feature explanations and use-case framing.

**Suze Orman's Must-Have Documents**
What to model: how a known financial professional sells a documents product without funneling to advisory services. Suze's positioning is "I built this so you have it" — pure publishing-product framing.
Note: skip the personality-driven hype; pull the structure.

**The Big Book of Everything** (originally a paper binder product)
What to model: the original framing of "everything your family needs to know is in one place." Simple, clear, durable positioning that's been copied dozens of times because it works.

### For the community ($29-$49/month)

**Morningstar Premium membership pages**
What to model: how a financial education subscription is sold to retirees and pre-retirees in a regulated environment. Calm, authoritative, conversion-focused without hype.

**James Clear's 3-2-1 Newsletter and book launches**
What to model: how a known author markets a community/newsletter without funneling to coaching or services. Clear sells books and newsletters; advisory work isn't the path. That's the model.

**Ramit Sethi / I Will Teach You to Be Rich**
What to model: gold standard for educational financial product marketing that converts at scale without selling securities. His sales pages, book copy, and course launches are widely studied for good reason. Energy is higher than Thomas's register, but the structural moves are excellent.

**Morgan Housel / The Psychology of Money**
What to model: how a financial author builds an audience and sells books through pure quality of thinking, not hype. Worth studying every blog post on collabfund.com for voice and positioning.

### For YouTube content that converts to community/products

**Erin Talks Money** (YouTube)
What to model: conversational financial education that doesn't pretend to be expert talking down to amateur. The "we're figuring this out together" register, but with real expertise underneath.

**Rob Berger** (YouTube)
What to model: retirement education from a former financial blogger. Calm, specific, low production value but high content value. Proves you don't need cinematic production to grow in this niche.

**James Conole / Root Financial** (YouTube)
What to model: an actual fee-only financial planner doing YouTube content that grows his channel without crossing into specific advice. Worth studying his hooks, titles, and video structures.

**Wade Pfau** (academic and YouTube content)
What to model: how to talk about retirement income and annuities (including FIAs) at a high level without product hype. Wade's research-backed approach is closer to Thomas's bucket-planning philosophy than almost any other public voice in retirement income.

### For book sales pages and book back-cover copy

**The Bogleheads' Guide to Investing** (Amazon page and back cover)
What to model: how a personal finance book is positioned for a buyer who doesn't want to be sold to.

**The Simple Path to Wealth (JL Collins)**
What to model: book description, Amazon A+ content, and the way the book is sold as a finished thing rather than a funnel into anything else.

**Die With Zero (Bill Perkins)**
What to model: provocative-but-defensible positioning. Strong opinion, clear book, doesn't lead anywhere except to another book purchase.

## Tier 2: Structural patterns from outside finance

These are sources where the SUBJECT is irrelevant but the STRUCTURE is gold.

### For long-form sales pages

**Basecamp / 37signals product pages and book pages**
What to model: how to sell software and books with calm, opinionated, anti-hype copy. The "ReWork" and "It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work" sales pages are masterclasses in low-pressure conversion.

**Penguin Random House book product pages** (general fiction and non-fiction)
What to model: how books are sold by publishers. Author bio placement, blurb structure, "praise for" sections done with restraint.

**Substack writers with paid subscriptions** (Bari Weiss, Matt Yglesias, Lenny Rachitsky)
What to model: how individual creators sell paid subscriptions to ongoing content. Particularly relevant for the community.

### For email sequences

**Seth Godin's email subscription sequences and book launches**
What to model: low-pressure, high-substance email marketing that respects the reader.

**Ramit Sethi's launch sequences**
What to model: structural moves and pacing across a multi-email launch.

## How to use these swipe files

When stuck on structure or pattern:
1. Identify what's not working (hero headline? value stack? FAQ?)
2. Pull up the equivalent section from 2-3 swipe files in the relevant tier
3. Note the structural moves they make — what's first, what's second, what's the transition
4. Apply that structure to TCA's product in Thomas's voice

Do NOT:
- Copy phrasing or sentences
- Mimic the voice of the swipe file (each has its own voice that won't fit Thomas)
- Reuse specific value propositions or bonuses

DO:
- Steal structural patterns
- Note what they include vs. what they leave out
- Pay attention to pacing and where they place the price/CTA

## Adding to this list

When a new high-converting source is identified that fits TCA's profile (financial education, family-organization, regulated-adjacent), add it here. Document:
- Why it fits (audience, regulatory profile, aesthetic)
- What to specifically model from it
- Anything to specifically NOT borrow

Keep the list curated. A swipe file collection that includes everything is worse than one that includes the right things.
