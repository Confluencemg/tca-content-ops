# TCA Sales Copy Skill

## Purpose

Produce sales copy for Thomas Clark Advisor (TCA) brand publishing products that:
1. Sounds like Thomas, not generic AI financial copy
2. Converts on the merits of the offer, not on manufactured urgency or hype
3. Maintains the standalone-publishing posture (no advisory funnel language)
4. Passes compliance scrutiny for a Series 65 IAR operating a separate publishing entity

## Scope: Brand-level, not entity-level

This skill is for the **Thomas Clark Advisor brand** specifically. While TCA operates under Confluence Media Group LLC, CMG also includes other unrelated properties (e.g., Built In The Dark) with completely different compliance, voice, and aesthetic requirements. Those properties have or will have their own sales copy skills.

This skill is NOT for:
- Built In The Dark or any other non-financial CMG property — use `bitd-sales-copy` or the relevant brand-specific skill
- Confluence Capital Management advisory marketing — different entity, different regulatory analysis, different voice
- Internal documents, strategy work, or anything not designed to sell publicly

If a request is ambiguous about which brand it's for, ask Thomas before applying this skill.

## When to use this skill

Use whenever Thomas is producing public-facing copy designed to sell or convert. Includes:

- Gumroad product pages
- Sales pages on thomasclarkadvisor.com
- KDP book descriptions and back-cover copy
- Amazon A+ content
- Course landing pages
- Community sales pages
- Lead magnet landing pages
- Product launch email sequences (when sequence drives to a sales page)
- Pinterest pin descriptions for products
- Affiliate brief copy
- Press kit product descriptions

Do NOT use for: blog posts (use blog voice), educational YouTube scripts (use video voice), client-facing advisory communications (different entity, different rules), internal documents or strategy work.

## Core Principles (apply every time)

### 1. Standalone Publishing Posture
The product being sold is a publishing product, not a path to advisory services. Public copy must reflect this:

- Author bio mentions Series 65 IAR credential as professional background ONLY (like a physician disclosing MD credentials in a health book)
- NO calls-to-action driving to advisory services
- NO "schedule a call" language anywhere on product pages
- NO "qualify for a consultation" funnel language
- Email capture is for the publishing list (more products, community, blog content), not for advisory lead gen
- If buyers organically discover Thomas is also an advisor, that's fine — design intent stops at the publishing business

### 2. Calm-Confident-Direct Register
Default tone characteristics:

- Direct without being aggressive
- Confident without being braggy
- Educational without being pedantic
- Empathetic without being saccharine
- Strong opinions stated plainly, never hedged into mush
- Conversational rhythm, not corporate or stiff
- Short sentences mixed with longer ones for cadence
- Speaks TO the reader, not AT the demographic

What to avoid:
- "BUT WAIT THERE'S MORE" stack-and-close pressure tactics
- Manufactured scarcity ("only 7 spots left!")
- Doomsday hooks ("the coming collapse will...")
- Implied performance promises of any kind
- Testimonial-heavy structures (regulatory issue + aesthetically wrong for the buyer)
- Fake urgency timers
- "Secret" framing for things that aren't actually secret
- Em-dash overuse, AI-isms, and the rhythm patterns that read as machine-generated

### 3. Voice Calibration
Pull voice from cumulative context:
- Thomas's pattern from our daily conversations (direct, opinionated, conversational, 18+ years of experience speaking)
- Blog content on thomasclarkadvisor.com when available as reference
- Specific corrections Thomas has made on prior copy
- Never freeze into a fixed template — adapt for the channel and product

When uncertain about voice on a specific section, draft it, flag it, and ask Thomas to redline. Voice fidelity calibrates through correction over iterations 1-5; by iteration 6+ the redline rate should drop into noise.

### 4. Mandatory Compliance Final Pass
Every piece of copy gets the compliance filter (see references/compliance-filter.md) before delivery. No exceptions.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Creative Director Interview

Before drafting any major piece of copy, ask Thomas 3-5 specific questions to pull the material that makes the copy land. Tailor questions to the product and the moment. Examples:

- "Who's the specific person you're imagining buying this — not the demographic, the actual one person?"
- "What does the buyer secretly fear about NOT having this?"
- "What's the conversation you'd want them to have with their spouse after they buy it?"
- "What's the story behind why you built this — what was the moment you knew it needed to exist?"
- "What's the wrong way to think about this product that you want to correct?"
- "If a buyer reads this page and DOESN'T buy, what would you want them to walk away believing anyway?"

Skip this step ONLY if Thomas says "just draft it" or if the request is clearly a small section edit.

### Step 2: Framework Selection

Pick the structural frameworks based on the piece. See references/framework-selection.md for the decision tree. Default selections for most products:

- **Overall narrative arc**: StoryBrand (buyer is hero, Thomas is guide, product is the plan)
- **Offer construction & value stacking**: Hormozi's Value Equation (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood ÷ Time Delay × Effort)
- **Hero headline awareness level**: Schwartz (most binder buyers are problem-aware)
- **Section openings**: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) for short sections
- **FAQ / objection handling**: Brunson's three false-belief patterns (vehicle, internal, external)
- **Positioning**: Brunson's new-opportunity test (is this a new vehicle or just a better version?)

### Step 3: Swipe File Reference Layer

When stuck on structure or pattern, reference the curated swipe files in references/swipe-files.md. These are sources to MODEL structure from, not copy from. The list deliberately excludes Agora-style financial publishing and info-marketer hype — those convert on tactics that don't fit Thomas's product, audience, or regulatory status.

### Step 4: Draft

Write the copy. Apply voice calibration throughout. Use modular structure so individual sections can be regenerated without rewriting the whole page.

Standard sales page sections (adapt as needed):
1. **Hero** — headline + subhead + visual + primary CTA
2. **Problem section** — what the buyer is struggling with, in their language
3. **Solution introduction** — what this product is, briefly
4. **The story / why this exists** — origin, credibility, why Thomas built it
5. **What's included** — concrete deliverables, not vague promises
6. **Who it's for / who it's not for** — qualifies the buyer
7. **FAQ / objection handling** — vehicle, internal, external concerns
8. **Author bio with disclosure** — Series 65 IAR mentioned, no advisory CTA
9. **Final CTA** — buy button + price + what happens next
10. **Footer disclaimers** — per the Altitude-reviewed baseline

### Step 5: Compliance Final Pass

Run every piece through references/compliance-filter.md before delivery. Flag anything that fails.

### Step 6: Annotated Delivery

Deliver the copy with a brief "framework notes" section at the end explaining:
- Which frameworks were applied where
- Which voice patterns were pulled in
- Anything Thomas should consider redlining
- Compliance flags (none, or specific items to review)

This is teaching mode — Thomas builds his own marketing fluency over time while we ship fast.

## Common Failure Modes (avoid these)

1. **Importing Brunson's webinar mechanics wholesale** — Perfect Webinar, Stack-and-close, "moral obligation to sell hard" framing. Wrong register, wrong audience, regulatory risk.

2. **Hyping the offer to compensate for unclear positioning** — if the headline needs three exclamation points and a flame emoji, the offer isn't clear. Fix the positioning, not the volume.

3. **Generic financial copy** — "Take control of your financial future" type phrases. Thomas doesn't talk like this. Buyers tune it out instantly.

4. **Hidden advisory funnel language** — "Schedule a call to see if you qualify," "Discover if working with us is right for you." This is the line that converts a publishing product into a regulated advertisement. Never appears in CMG product copy.

5. **Testimonial-heavy structures** — even legitimate reviews trigger Marketing Rule scrutiny when the author is a Series 65 IAR. Use sparingly and with proper disclosure language. When in doubt, omit.

6. **Implied performance promises** — "What if you could retire 5 years earlier?" type framing is a Marketing Rule problem. Stick to what the product DOES, not what outcomes it produces.

7. **AI-tells in the prose** — em-dash clusters, "it's not just X, it's Y" rhythms, list-of-three constructions repeated, "in today's world" openings, "let's dive in" transitions. Voice check catches these.
